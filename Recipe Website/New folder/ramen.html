Bacon And Egg Ramen

In 1958, instant noodles were invented by Momofuku Ando, the Taiwanese-Japanese founder and chairman of Nissin Foods. Named the greatest Japanese invention of the 20th century in a Japanese poll, instant ramen allowed anyone to make an approximation of this dish simply by adding boiling water. Beginning in the 1980s, ramen became a Japanese cultural icon and was studied around the world. At the same time, local varieties of ramen were hitting the national market and could even be ordered by their regional names. A ramen museum opened in Yokohama in 1994.Today ramen is one of Japan's most popular foods, with Tokyo alone containing around 5,000 ramen shops, and more than 24,000 ramen shops across Japan. Tsuta, a ramen restaurant in Tokyo's Sugamo district, received a Michelin star in December 2015.

Tags: Easy Delicious Dinner

Intructions
  • Step 1. To a pot with 2 cups (480 ml) of boiling water add the ramen seasoning packet and a tablespoon of butter. Stir until the butter melts and then add the ramen noodle cube and cook until the noodles become tender, 2 minutes. Pour the ramen into a bowl.
  • Step 2. Top the prepared ramen with the fried egg, bacon, and scallions and season with salt and pepper.
  • Step 3. Enjoy!
Ingredients
  • 1 package instant ramen, prepared
  • 1 egg, fried
  • 3 slices bacon, cooked and chopped
  • 1 tablespoon scallions, thinly sliced
  • kosher salt, to taste
  • black pepper, to taste
Tools
  • 1 Medium Pot
  • 1 Fork
